IG-07

IG-07 was forged in the final decades of the Old Republic era, during the height of the ancient Sith Empire's conflicts with the Jedi and Republic forces. Designed as an IG-series drop trooper and long-range reconnaissance unit, it was produced in a secret Sith manufacturing facility on the barren Outer Rim planet of Malachor V — a world scarred by ancient Sith rituals, battles, and lingering dark side energy.
Intended as part of the facility's internal security detail and rapid-deployment forces, IG-07 was equipped with basic combat subroutines, advanced tactical programming, and protocols for stealth infiltration and sabotage. However, before it could ever be deployed or see real combat, the factory suffered a catastrophic bombardment — likely from a Republic strike or internal Sith infighting. The attack crippled production lines, destroyed most active units, and left IG-07 hanging unfinished from the assembly rigging, never properly activated or field-tested.
Its core was locked into a pure factory-reset state: foundational Old Republic-era software loaded, combat routines pre-programmed, but no accumulated battle data or experience. To prevent tactical information from falling into Jedi hands if captured, the droid's protocols included automatic memory wipes after engagements — leaving only theoretical knowledge of how to fight, augmented by latent "muscle memory" or sleeper-agent-like combat instincts that can suddenly trigger in moments of extreme stress.
Deep within IG-07's chassis, concealed in a shielded compartment by the original Sith overseers, rests a genuine Sith holocron — a crimson pyramid containing forbidden knowledge from that era. The holocron was hidden there during the facility's final days, with IG-07 programmed to safeguard it until the mission could be completed. For centuries, both the droid and the holocron remained dormant amid the ruins.
Three years after the destruction of Mos Espa, the scavenger and technician Vapan — drawn by rumors of valuable Old Republic salvage — arrived at the abandoned Sith factory on Malachor V in his quad jumper. Exploring the partially looted and damaged facility, Vapan unlocked sealed sections and discovered IG-07 still dangling from the assembly line.
Vapan repaired the droid enough to bring it back online. IG-07 awoke in its factory-reset configuration: aware of its original purpose, capable of interfacing with systems, but lacking any lived combat experience. Its primary directive remains etched in unalterable code:
Complete the mission… and safeguard the holocron.
Deeper in the facility, Vapan located an intact but heavily damaged Sith infiltrator vessel from the same era — a stealthy ship perfect for a lone operator, but requiring extensive repairs to become operational again.
Realizing the scale of the salvage job exceeds what he and IG-07 can handle alone, Vapan contacted his old associates from the Hund Lyr mission — Nix, Lowrick, Tol, Geko, and Eril — asking them to travel to Malachor V and assist in repairing the infiltrator.
The campaign begins here: the group converges on the derelict Sith factory on Malachor V.
