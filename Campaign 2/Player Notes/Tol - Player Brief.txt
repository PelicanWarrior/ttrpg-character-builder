Tol - Player Brief

Home:
Tol was raised in Nabat's Lantern, a small Twi'lek enclave hidden among the red stone gullies of Ryloth's Bright Lands. It was poor, watchful, and tight-knit. Everyone worked, everyone kept watch, and survival depended on protecting each other.

Parents:
Bilmil, Tol's father, was a hunter and watchman. He was not a man of many words, but he taught Tol patience, awareness, and restraint.
Trethi, Tol's mother, was a healer and storyteller. She taught Tol compassion, memory, and the belief that strength means nothing if it is not used in defense of others.

The fall of Nabat's Lantern:
When Tol was still young, the enclave was raided by slavers working for the Zygerrian Slave Empire. The attack shattered the community. Some died. Some were taken. Some vanished into the wastes. Tol never learned the full truth of who lived and who did not.

Life after the raid:
Determined never to be helpless again, Tol eventually joined the Iron Vulture Company, an Outer Rim mercenary outfit. There he learned discipline, battlefield tactics, and how easily good intentions could be corrupted by bad orders.

Turning point:
Tol left the company after a disastrous mission that cost innocent lives, including children. Since then, he has tried to use his strength to protect rather than simply survive.

What still matters to Tol:
- The memory of Nabat's Lantern
- The lessons of Bilmil and Trethi
- Hatred of slavers, especially Zygerrians and those who work for them
- Guilt over his mercenary past
- The unresolved question of what became of his people
