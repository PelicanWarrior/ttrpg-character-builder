Iron Vulture Company

Type:
Outer Rim mercenary company

Reputation:
Professional, reliable under fire, morally flexible when paid enough

Overview:
The Iron Vulture Company is a mid-sized mercenary outfit that operates across the Outer Rim, taking contracts as convoy escorts, security specialists, shock troops, recovery teams, and enforcers. They are not ideological. They follow the money, then justify the job afterward.

Why Tol joined:
Tol wanted strength, training, and a way to make sure no one could ever destroy his home again while he stood helpless. The Iron Vultures offered structure, harsh discipline, and a place for anger to become skill.

How the company works:
- Units are organized into claws, each led by a veteran sergeant
- Payment is split by rank and casualty risk
- Failure is punished harshly, but visible weakness is punished worse
- Civilians matter only when written into the contract

Known Tol-era members:
- Sergeant Vara Sorn: human female, claw leader, pragmatic and unsentimental. She valued Tol because he stayed calm and did not break under pressure.
- Jek Marr: human male heavy gunner, loud, loyal in a fight, one of the few squadmates Tol trusted.
- Siva Noret: Mirialan female scout, sharp-eyed and dry-humored, often warned Tol when a contract smelled wrong.
- Droven Kalt: human male field officer and ambitious opportunist. He believed fear was more useful than respect and became one of the men Tol came to despise.

Tol's enemy from that time:
Droven Kalt should be treated as a recurring personal antagonist. He survived the mission that broke Tol's faith in the company and sees Tol's refusal to obey dirty orders as weakness disguised as conscience.

Breaking point:
Tol left after a contract degraded into a slaughter of innocents, including children caught in the crossfire. Whether the disaster came from incompetence, greed, or deliberate betrayal is a question the GM can answer later.

Current use in campaign:
The Iron Vulture Company can be used as:
- A source of old allies or bitter enemies
- A connection to black-market employers and warlords
- A trail leading Tol back into the kind of life he tried to leave
- The origin of debts, rumors, and unfinished business
