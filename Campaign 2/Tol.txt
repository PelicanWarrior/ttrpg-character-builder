Tol

Tol’s Backstory

Tol was born on Ryloth, a harsh and unforgiving world where survival often demanded more than just strength. He was raised in Nabat's Lantern, a small Twi'lek enclave hidden among the red stone gullies beyond the better-known settlements of the Bright Lands. Life there was shaped by scarcity, dust storms, and the constant threat of raids from slavers and opportunists who saw isolated Twi'lek communities as easy prey. Tol's father, Bilmil, was a steady-handed hunter and watchman who taught him patience, discipline, and how to read danger in the movement of the wind and sand. His mother, Trethi, was the heart of the enclave, a healer and storyteller who taught Tol that strength meant nothing if it was not used to protect others. From them, Tol learned honor early, but he also learned that ideals alone would not keep his people alive.

When he was still young, Nabat's Lantern was devastated in a brutal raid carried out by hired guns and slavers in service to the Zygerrian Slave Empire, a scourge long feared across Twi'lek space. The attackers came fast, striking at night with stun weapons, fire, and shock collars, killing those who resisted and taking others in chains. Tol witnessed neighbors, friends, and kin fall in the chaos. Bilmil fought to hold the line while Trethi helped children and the wounded flee into the rocks. Tol survived, but the enclave was broken, scattered, and burned. In the confusion that followed, he never learned the fate of every survivor, only that the life he knew was gone. The memory of that night carved deep scars into him, and from then on he swore he would never be helpless again.

Driven by a fierce desire to protect, Tol joined the Iron Vulture Company in his teens, a hard-bitten mercenary outfit that sold its strength as escorts, enforcers, and battlefield specialists across the Outer Rim. Among those he served beside were Sergeant Vara Sorn, a human woman and pragmatic claw leader who forged discipline into every order; Jek Marr, a human man, loud but dependable heavy gunner, and one of the few squadmates Tol came to trust; Siva Noret, a Mirialan woman and sharp-eyed scout whose instincts often told her when a contract had gone rotten; and Droven Kalt, a human man and ambitious field officer whose cruelty and hunger for advancement put him at odds with everything Tol had been taught. Tol quickly rose through the ranks, valued for his calm demeanor, strength, and unwavering loyalty. But his growing reputation wasn’t enough to shield him from a mission gone horribly wrong—an operation that resulted in innocent lives lost, including children caught in the crossfire.

Haunted by guilt, Tol left the squad, rejecting the cold brutality he once embodied. He vowed to use his skills to defend those who couldn’t defend themselves, carrying the weight of his past but refusing to be defined by it.
