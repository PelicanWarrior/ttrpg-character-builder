Vapan the subtle

Vapan was raised on Carinthia, a remote moon of Dathomir, among an ancient, relic lineage of the Nightsisters. Unlike the magick-wielding witches of the main clans on Dathomir itself, this isolated group on Carinthia focused on practical, non-magical skills — training their young as elite contract killers and operatives for hire. From childhood, Vapan was groomed to serve as an assassin and bounty hunter, carrying out the Nightsisters' contracts (or those of whoever paid them) with cold efficiency.
Throughout his early life, he honed his talents in bounty hunting, tracking, killing, and survival across the Outer Rim. He became a reliable blade-for-hire, known for getting the job done without questions.
Eventually, Zorba the Hutt (or his organization) contracted the Nightsisters to outfit and deploy Vapan for a high-profile assassination: eliminate a target named Hund Lyr. During this mission on Tatooine, Vapan crossed paths with Nix, Lowrick, Tol, Geko, and Eril from the previous campaign. The contract succeeded, earning him a substantial payout in credits.
After the mission, Nix, Lowrick, Eril, and the others dropped Vapan off at a space station. With his earnings, he purchased a cheap, second-hand quad jumper — a rugged but unremarkable ship suited for a lone operator.
In the three-year gap between the previous campaign and the current one, Vapan heard rumors of an abandoned Sith Empire factory from the Old Republic era — a long-forgotten manufacturing site hidden on the barren Outer Rim planet of Malachor V. Drawn by the promise of valuable salvage, he flew there in his quad jumper.
Upon arrival, he found the facility already partially looted or damaged: most security drones had been destroyed or deactivated by previous opportunistic scavengers. As a skilled technician with a knack for computers and systems, Vapan explored deeper than others had managed. He unlocked a sealed section of the vault.
While searching the factory for useful components, Vapan located IG-07 (also called Kaiser) — a damaged IG-series drop trooper droid still hanging from the assembly line / factory rigging. The droid had been part of the facility's security detail, intended for deployment but never activated due to a bombardment or attack that hit before it saw action. It remained in pure factory-reset state, with basic combat programming loaded but no field experience.
Vapan repaired IG-07 enough to bring the droid back online. Because of the reset protocols (designed to wipe tactical data after each engagement to prevent capture and interrogation by Jedi forces), IG-07 retains only foundational Old Republic-era software and combat subroutines — including flashes of "muscle memory" or sleeper-agent-like combat instincts that can trigger in high-stress moments. He has theoretical knowledge of how to fight but lacks real battle scars or accumulated experience.
Deeper in the facility, Vapan also discovered an intact Sith infiltrator vessel from the Old Republic era — a stealthy, powerful ship that could change everything for a lone operator like him. The vessel is in poor condition: heavily damaged, systems offline, and requiring extensive repairs to become flight-worthy again.
Realizing the job is too big for one person, Vapan reached out to his old contacts from the Hund Lyr mission — Nix, Lowrick, Tol, Geko, and Eril — asking them to come to Malachor V and help him salvage and repair the infiltrator.
The campaign begins here: the group has just arrived (or is arriving) at the abandoned Sith factory on Malachor V, where Vapan and the newly reactivated IG-07 await them. The derelict infiltrator sits waiting, full of potential secrets — including the Sith holocron still concealed inside IG-07's chassis.
