Nabat's Lantern

Type:
Small Twi'lek enclave on Ryloth

Region:
Hidden among the red stone gullies beyond the Bright Lands

Overview:
Nabat's Lantern was a modest, semi-hidden Twi'lek settlement built into wind-cut canyon walls and sheltered caves. It was named for the old lantern tower that stood at the mouth of the main approach, its light shielded and directed by mirrored plates so trusted travelers could find the enclave without exposing it to raiders from afar.

Appearance:
- Adobe and stone dwellings built into the canyon wall
- Rope bridges and narrow ledges linking upper homes and lookout points
- Moisture traps and cisterns tucked into shaded rock faces
- A central fire court used for meals, stories, and mourning rites
- A concealed lantern beacon used only at agreed times

Culture:
Nabat's Lantern valued mutual protection, memory, and practical service. Children were taught to fight only when needed, but every child learned how to hide, carry water, keep watch, and signal danger. Stories of ancestors and songs of endurance were treated as survival tools, not luxuries.

Customs:
- The Lantern Watch: each household took turns maintaining the hidden beacon and outer lookouts
- Red Dust Remembrance: after every dust storm, names of the dead were spoken aloud so no one would be lost to silence
- Shared Table Night: once every ten-day, all food was pooled and redistributed so no household faced hunger alone
- First Blade Oath: adolescents receiving their first real weapon swore to defend the enclave before seeking glory

Important places:
- The Lantern Tower: old signal post and lookout perch
- Trethi's Court: healer's shelter and herb drying space
- Bilmil's Shelf: a narrow watch ledge used to track movement through the gullies
- The Hollow Vault: hidden store chamber for food, water, medicine, and weapons

Current status:
Destroyed and scattered in a Zygerrian-backed slave raid. Tol knows the enclave fell, but not the fate of every survivor.
