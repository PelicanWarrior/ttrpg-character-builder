Scrapper's Mercy, Quelith

Type:
Salvage outpost settlement

Moon:
Quelith — a tidally locked, geologically stable moon orbiting Malachor V

Region:
Rimward shelf of Quelith's sunlit face, built inside and around a collapsed ore-transfer gantry salvaged from an old mining operation

Overview:
Scrapper's Mercy is a rough, functional settlement of salvage crews, mechanics, slicers, and drifters who make a living pulling value from dead warzones and ruined facilities. It is equal parts market, machine yard, and pressure cooker. Nothing is wasted, nothing is free, and every favor has a price.

The settlement clings to life through jury-rigged shields, patched oxygen processors, and strict salvage claims. Rows of welded shacks and scaffold decks are built from ship plating and factory scrap, all centered around a floodlit trade spine called the Strip. Loud generators, cutting torches, and cargo lifters run day and night.

Quelith itself is barren and airless away from the settlement's pressure envelope, but orbitally stable. It sits far enough from Malachor V to avoid the worst of the planet's tectonic cracking and electrical storms — though the planet still dominates the sky, visibly fractured and glowing with deep-core fires along its fault lines. Occasional debris from Malachor V's upper atmosphere drifts into low Quelith orbit, which salvagers sometimes intercept for raw materials.

Atmosphere and Tone:
- Constant grinder noise, sparks, and radio chatter
- Smell of hot metal, coolant, and ozone
- Frequent arguments over salvage rights and debt
- Armed peacekeepers hired by the dockmaster to keep the market from turning into open war

How It Fits Session 1:
Scrapper's Mercy is the nearest practical source of parts, fuel cells, and specialist labor for repairing the Sith infiltrator on Malachor V's surface. The party shuttles down from Quelith to reach the wreck, or travels up from the surface to the moon for resupply. Every transaction on Quelith attracts attention from rivals, brokers, and spies — and making too many trips between the moon and the surface will eventually draw questions about what is down there.

What The Party Can Find Here:
- Ship components and improvised replacement parts
- Black-market tools and slicing support
- Local guides who know safe and unsafe approach routes to old ruins
- Rumors about other groups searching Sith-era sites
