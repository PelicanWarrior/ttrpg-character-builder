Eril Fling

Born on Tatooine in the Dune Sea to the Jaws clan Fling Flang.

Eril Fling was raised in the Utinni way, to expertly negotiate and scavenge and repair various robots, machinery and weaponry, developing a customised Ion Pistol called the Ionfang his pride and joy, all on the sandcrawler: Dunebreaker
Developing his own BD unite BD-N8 with components of various droids making it a valuable asset to his sand crawler.

Eril grew up surrounded by a large, boisterous family of 7 brothers and 5 sisters, all raised under the watchful eye of their mother, Chumu Fling.

Chumu was a respected figure in the clan, wise in the old Jawa ways and gifted with a shamanic intuition for sensing hidden tech in the dunes. She was destined to become the clan's shamanic leader — the spiritual guide who oversaw rituals, interpreted omens in the sandstorms, and kept the family's ancestral knowledge alive. Her calm, measured "Utinni" carried weight; when she spoke, even the rowdiest Jawas listened.
Eril's sisters were:
Rabam Fling — The eldest sister, sharp-tongued and fearless, often leading scouting parties into Tusken territory. She had a talent for quick repairs on the move and a habit of collecting shiny trinkets as "lucky charms."
Elenal Fling — Quiet and observant, Elenal specialized in electronics and circuitry. She could rewire a fried motivator blindfolded and was known for her patience in teaching younger Jawas the finer points of droid reprogramming.
Kofek Fling — The family's chief haggler, Kofek had a silver tongue (or glowing yellow eyes, at least) that could talk moisture farmers into paying triple for scrap. Her laughter was infectious, even through the hooded robes.
Thedic Fling — Adventurous and bold, Thedic loved exploring deep into abandoned moisture farms or crashed ships, always returning with rare components — and the occasional scar from a sandstorm.
Hanim Fling — The youngest sister, still in training, Hanim showed early promise as a miniaturist mechanic, building tiny gadgets from discarded screws and wires. She idolized Eril and often tagged along on his repairs.
His brothers included:
Dos Fling — The strongest of the brothers, Dos handled heavy lifting and sandcrawler maintenance. He was gruff but protective, always ready to defend the family with his gaffi stick if Tusken Raiders drew near.
Veezz Fling — A natural inventor like Eril, Veezz specialized in weapon modifications and once jury-rigged a sandcrawler turret from old podracer parts.
Krus Fling — The negotiator of the brothers, Krus had a calm demeanor and a keen eye for spotting fakes during trades. He often mediated disputes between clan members.
Penk Fling — Energetic and impulsive, Penk was the clan's fastest scout, zipping across the dunes on a salvaged swoop bike to spot salvage before rival clans.
Bee Fling — The quiet tinkerer, Bee preferred working alone in the sandcrawler's workshop, restoring ancient droids to perfect condition.
Tex Fling — Bold and loud, Tex loved storytelling around the clan's glow-rods at night, embellishing every salvage haul into epic tales of adventure.
Zubwit Fling — The youngest brother, Zubwit was clever and mischievous, always pulling pranks with reprogrammed mouse droids or fake "treasure maps" to lure siblings into chores.
With such a large family, the sandcrawler was always alive with chatter, clanking tools, and the hum of repaired droids. Chumu prepared Eril and his siblings for the day she would ascend as shaman, teaching them the old chants and the secrets of the desert's hidden tech.
But Eril's path diverged when opportunity — and obligation — called. Service to the Hutts came in the form of a lucrative contract: provide specialized scavenging and repair expertise for one of their operations. Leaving the Dune Sea behind, Eril departed the family sandcrawler with BD-N8 at his side, the Ionfang holstered, and a promise to return one day with riches and stories. His mother Chumu blessed him with a quiet "Utinni" of farewell, knowing the desert always called its children home.

He gained the favour of various Hutts fixing necessary equipment for their palaces and speeders being referred to the service of Zorba the Hutt becoming a favourite Jawa to deal with business dealing with scavenged goods and contraband weapons.

After one more commission Zorba agreed to allow Eril to branch out in his new found crew and friends.
Zorba commissioned Eril as part of the crew to secure a ship for a valued client which is where our adventure really begins.

After securing the ship for the client, capturing a young krait dragon and freeing the town of Dustfall from a molevolent ruler he finished his contract but at what cost?

The weapon that was secured for the ship wiped out the whole city of Mos Espa under the command of Jedi.

Three years later and with stronger bonds with his crew he seeks to never let something so awful happen again no matter how scary a Jedi may seem to be.

The crew has been having strange dreams and visions since that day, what does it all mean?
