Nyx

Nix grew up in her father Kelnan's small repair hangar on the frozen moon of Pantora, in the remote settlement of Ro Station — a modest outpost where business struggled amid the cold winds and isolation, far from the bustling capital city. Surrounded by the constant smell of coolant, burned wiring, and ozone from arc welders, she learned to use a hydrospanner before she could properly read. Machines were reliable; people were not. Stubborn and sharp-tongued, Nix preferred the quiet logic of circuits and engines to the unpredictability of conversation.
Her mother, Jo, had died many years earlier when Nix was still a small child. The circumstances of her death remain a complete mystery to Nix — Kelnan refused to speak of it, ever. Any question about Jo was met with the same reaction: his face would shutter like a blast door sealing, his voice would drop to a flat tone, and he would redirect the conversation to a faulty motivator, a stubborn repulsor coil, or the need to clean the diagnostic tools. Over time, Nix stopped asking. The silence became a permanent fixture in their home, heavier than any unspoken grief. She never learned whether it was an accident, illness, violence, or something else entirely. All she knows is that Jo was gone, and Kelnan carried the knowledge alone — and took it with him when he died.
Kelnan's Hangar was a haven of organized chaos: stacks of salvaged parts, flickering diagnostic screens, and the low hum of repulsorlifts being tuned. Kelnan was a patient, skilled mechanic who taught Nix everything he knew. He dreamed of expanding the shop one day, but the Outer Rim's harsh economy kept them scraping by.
One brutal winter cycle, a heavily damaged freighter limped into Kelnan's Hangar for emergency repairs. The crew — four Black Sun operatives (a cold-eyed Rodian sniper, a hulking Trandoshan enforcer, a scarred Weequay pirate, and a smooth-talking Falleen lieutenant) — were secretly transporting crates of illegal weapons for pirate gangs. While crawling through the hull to patch a ruptured coolant line, Nix discovered the hidden cargo. When the crew realized she had seen too much, things turned deadly in seconds.
Blaster fire erupted inside the confined hangar. Kelnan stepped in to protect his daughter and was cut down shielding her. In the chaos, Nix slipped away, hot-wired the Black Sun freighter’s systems, and sealed the crew inside the hangar bay. She stranded the four operatives on Pantora and blasted off into the void before they could break free.
The escape was rough — alarms screaming, shields flickering — but the ship carried her far from the moon and the life she had lost. Days later, she slipped into the black-market docks of the Corellian Sector on Nar Shaddaa, sold the stolen freighter to a shady ship-breaker for a fraction of its value, and disappeared into the Smuggler’s Moon with a pocket full of credits and a new identity.
Not long after, a separate mercenary crew operating in Hutt-controlled space intercepted her drifting transport. They boarded intending to strip the vessel for parts and eject the “stowaway” — until their own hyperdrive failed mid-jump. Nix diagnosed and repaired it in minutes under fire from pursuing pirates. Impressed by her skill and nerve, the crew let her stay as their onboard mechanic.
The crew was a rough but effective outfit under the command of Qas Khulc, a female Weequay captain whose leathery skin bore the scars of countless boarding actions and whose quiet, ritualistic demeanor hid a ruthless tactical mind. She led with few words and absolute authority, favoring vibro-axes in close quarters and expecting the same loyalty she gave to her people.
Her second-in-command in combat was Tortt Ma, a male Rodian with large, multifaceted eyes and a hunter’s instinct. Quick on the trigger and quicker to boast, Tortt served as the crew’s sniper and scout, always scanning for bounties, escape routes, or the next easy score.
Negla Dak, a female Trandoshan, provided the raw muscle and demolitions expertise. Hulking and reptilian, with scales that gleamed under ship lights and claws that could tear through durasteel, Negla lived for the hunt and the “points” her kills earned in the eyes of her Scorekeeper. She was terrifying in close quarters and always eager for a fight.
Rounding out the group was Tun Klub, a male Gamorrean brute with tusks, immense strength, and a surprisingly loyal heart once someone earned his respect. Not the brightest, but incredibly durable, Tun handled brute labor, door-busting, and serving as the crew’s intimidating wall during negotiations or boarding actions.
The mercenaries eventually returned to their employer, Zorba the Hutt. Working for a Hutt wasn’t honorable — Nix knew that — but it gave her access to ships, parts, and machines that needed fixing. Before long, the stubborn Pantoran mechanic became the crew’s invaluable lifeline: patching hull breaches, recalibrating weapons, and keeping their aging fleet in the fight. Qas Khulc came to rely on her more than she would admit, Tortt traded boasts for her repairs, Negla grunted approval at her quick fixes, and even Tun offered a rare, rumbling nod of respect when she kept the ship flying.
She carries the weight of the loss of Kelnan, her father — plus the knowledge that four Black Sun operatives are still out there, probably hunting the woman who stranded and robbed them.
